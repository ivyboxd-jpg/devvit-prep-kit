WHO
HAS THE
PROBLEM?

A good proposal clearly defines who has the problem, not just what the problem is.

If you build a brilliant solution for a vaguely defined audience, you will fail the selection gate. Specificity is survival. Target a real demographic, define their exact pain point, and build the smallest prototype that solves it.

Evaluation Matrix

Judges evaluate 5 minutes of your presentation, not 36 hours of code. A brilliant build fails if the pitch doesn't explain why it matters.
